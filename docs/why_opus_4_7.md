# Why Opus 4.7 Matters Here

Opus 4.7 is the visible scientist in this workflow.

Its role is not just generic coding assistance. It is the model that makes the judged artifact legible as a discovery loop.

## Opus 4.7 Responsibilities

- propose compact law families worth searching,
- suggest skeptic tests before we trust a candidate,
- explain why a surviving law is biologically interpretable, and
- keep the discovery narrative coherent from proposal to falsification to summary.

## Why The Workflow Is Opus-Centered

Many pipelines can brute-force equations. That alone is not the point of the hackathon.

This project is centered on an Opus 4.7 reasoning loop:

1. propose promising structures,
2. search concrete instances,
3. reject fragile fits,
4. retain an interpretable candidate,
5. explain what the surviving form may mean biologically.

The backstage stack can include Codex, overnight automation, or HPC. The judged narrative still keeps Opus 4.7 at the center of the scientific reasoning layer.

## What The Demo Should Prove

The demo should show that Opus 4.7 is useful where scientific discovery becomes hard:

- narrowing symbolic search to interpretable families,
- generating falsification plans instead of only celebrating fit,
- summarizing the surviving law in plain English, and
- making the discovery workflow portable to a second dataset.
